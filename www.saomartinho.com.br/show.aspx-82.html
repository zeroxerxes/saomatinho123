      SÃO MARTINHO NAMED INDUSTRY CHAMPION
      IN VALOR 1000 AWARDS FOR THE THIRD TIME

       

      São Paulo, September 6, 2022 - São Martinho won the Bioenergy category in the 2022 edition of the Valor 1000 Awards sponsored by the newspaper Valor Econômico in partnership with the Getúlio Vargas Foundation (FGV) and Serasa Experian. The awards ranks Brazil’s one thousand largest companies based on the best results and performance in 27 economic sectors (26 companies and 1 financial institution).

      In the awards ceremony hosted at Hotel Unique in São Paulo, on September 5, São Martinho was represented by its Chief Financial and Investor Relations Officer, Felipe Vicchiato.

      The Company was elected sector champion in the Valor 1000 for the third time. In 2017 and 2020, it won in the Sugar & Ethanol category (which in the 2022 edition was renamed Bioenergy), and in the latter edition also was named Valor Company (champion of sector champions).

      In addition to Felipe Vicchiato, the awards ceremony was attended by Maurício Krug Ometto, Chairman of the Board of São Martinho; Marcelo Campos Ometto, Council Member; Luciana Carvas, Human Resources, Health and Safety Officer; and Helder Gosling, Commercial and Logistics Officer.

      About Valor 1000

      Valor 1000 is the largest and most important yearbook of Brazilian companies. The publication brings exclusive indicators and a ranking of the 1,000 largest companies in the company by net revenue. 

      The Valor 1000 winners are based on the performance of the companies compiled in the ranking in accounting and financial criteria. For the ranking’s 22nd edition, 1,069 non-financial companies were analyzed by Valor and its partners Serasa Experian and the Center for Financial Studies of the São Paulo Business School of the Getúlio Vargas Foundation (FGV/CEF/EASP/FGVSP).

      This year’s edition also featured a change in the process for evaluating sector champions, with the three highest-ranking companies in the six financial accounting criteria moving on to the second phase, where they are evaluated by a committee of eight market specialists in Environment, Social and Governance (ESG) practices.
