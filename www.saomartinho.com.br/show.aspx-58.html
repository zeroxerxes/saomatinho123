      Electric Energy

      Sugarcane bagasse, the residue of the sugar and ethanol manufacturing process, is completely reused. The electric power provided by its burning feeds the mills and is still sold - a clean process that avoids the use of fossil fuels.

      Check below the process for the production of electricity.

       
       
       
       
       
       
       
       
       
      AGRICULTURAL PRODUCTION

      Soil preparation and correction, Planting, Fertilization, Fertigation, Pest Control, diseases and weeds, are some of the several operations that make up the cane production process, making available in quantity and quality suitable for harvesting

      HARVEST

      Our sugarcane fields are harvested through a mechanized process, using leading edge technologies such as high performance machines, high precision GPS, 4G communication, among others.

      TRANSPORT

      Then, high-capacity trucks transport the cane from the cane fields to the mills

      MILLING

      The sugarcane is then milled for the extraction of the broth, used in the manufacture of sugar and ethanol. This process also results in bagasse, intended for the production of bioelectricity.

      BROTH

      After milling, the sugarcane juice is separated from the bagasse and passed through heaters, decanters and filters for the removal of mineral impurities.

      ETHANOL AND SUGAR

      Then, the clarified broth is subjected to several processes that will transform it into ethanol and sugar.

      BAGASSE

      Besides the sugarcane juice, the milling process also produces the bagasse, which is reused.

      BOILER

      Bagasse is sent to feed the plant's boilers, where it is burned for steam generation.

      ENERGY

      Steam is transformed into thermal, mechanical and electrical energy. The latter is used for the own consumption of the plant and the surplus is sold.
